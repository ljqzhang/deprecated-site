    Kinbaku Skate Shoes

    I grew up in the Bay Area, where skate culture is pervasive. While my own abilities are limited to pushing around from Point A to Point B and the occassional baby ollie, I've always been fascinated by skating's combination of sheer athleticism and cultural impact.

    The Kinbaku Skate Shoe is a concept for a skate shoe that blends utilitarian sensibility with street-ready style. Combining visual cues from traditional Japanese rope bondage and speculative science fiction, the Kinbaku Skate Shoe is aesthetically divergent from existing skateboarding-oriented designs on the market.

    Created Spring 2016.

    Goals and Parameters

    There are plenty of iconic skate shoes, with Vans Sk8-Hi being the classic example. I wanted to push the boundaries of what people imagine to be a 'skate shoe' and design a fashion-oriented shoe that was still functional.

    However, there were design parameters I had to keep in mind:
    • The shoe should have a flat bottom
    • Suede is a preferable material due to its durability
    • The shoe should be comfortable and easy to put on and take off
    • Decorative elements should not hinder performance
